In 1869 a Russian scientist called Dmitri Mendeleev invented the periodic system that we still use today. Before him others had tried to 'order' the elements but their tables were incomplete or grouped elements together which weren't similar. Dmitri's table didn't include all of the elements because not all of them were known at the time. He left gaps for them though and could predict their properties based on the elements around them in his table.
